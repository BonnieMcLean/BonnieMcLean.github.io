About

I made this blog as a way to keep track of recipes I've made that turned out well. The main purpose of the site is that you can search for recipes based on (a) how much effort the dish was to make and (b) how rewarding the dish was to eat. All the recipes are tried and tested by me, and I've only included recipes that I would make again, so usually for each recipe the reward is always greater than or equal to the effort. However, in a few cases the effort may be greater than the reward, but the recipe is still included because it was enjoyable to make. This usually only happens with baking.

Most of the recipes on this site come from other (professional) cooking blogs, in which case I just provide the link to the recipe and a comment on any changes I made or why you should make it. However, a few of the recipes are my own, and in these cases the recipe is provided on the site.

Why cooking?

I think cooking is the best hobby. Assuming all goes to plan, it provides (fairly) instant gratification, and everyone needs to eat, so why not do it well.

Asian ingredients

Many of the dishes on here contain asian ingredients you may not be able to find in your local supermarket. If you're stuck on where to find them, one of my favourite food blogs provides a helpful list of asian supermarkets worldwide that you can use to search for supermarkets near you.
