Basic crumble

effort: 😫😫    reward: 😋😋😋

source: personal recipe (see below)

comments:

If you don't end up using all the crumble topping, you can freeze it and then you have it ready for whenever you want to make a crumble next time. Or make a double batch and freeze half for next time. Pictured here with homemade vanilla icecream

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 cup oats
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 150g butter, softened
  • 1tsp cinammon
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder (not essential but leads to better browning)
  • 1/2tsp cardamom (optional)
  • flaked almonds(optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180.
  2. Whisk together oats, flour, baking powder, sugar, and spices.
  3. Add butter and rub mixture together between your fingers until it gets breadcrumby.
  4. Use on top of poached fruit (with a little of the poaching liquid, you can add a bit of flour/cornflour to the poaching liquid if you want it more saucy), baking for 20/25 minutes.

Filling ideas

  • Green apple -- peeled, cored and chunked then soften it up on the stove with a bit of water, sugar and a squeeze of lemon juice, bit of flour, bit of ginger.
  • Rhubarb -- chunk it up, put it on baking paper on a tray and sprinkle over a generous amount of sugar, generous amount of lemon juice, lemon zest, bit of ginger, and if you want to be fancy you could get a vanilla pod and scrape on the seeds, also nice if you add some star anise, cardamom pods, then cover the tray/rhubarb with foil and cook at 200 degrees for around 20 minutes until it's softened.
  • Frozen berries -- just add some flour and sugar, or vanilla sugar if you want to be fancy, to them and mix it up with them before adding the crumble topping (for 100g frozen berries you need maybe 2 tsp sugar, 1 tsp flour or cornflour). 20 minutes at 200 degrees (see Nigella).
  • Unripe strawberries -- 1:10 ratio sugar:strawberries (use grams), do it in the oven at 200 for 30 minutes together with the crumble topping. It's nice if you add some ground almonds to the strawberries and flaked almonds in the crumble (see Nigella).
