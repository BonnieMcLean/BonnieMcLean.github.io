Granola

effort: 😫    reward: 😋😋

source: personal recipe (see below)

comments:

Once you've had homemade granola, it's really hard to go back to store-bought when you know this tastes SO much better and it's SUPER easy to make. I can't stand dried fruit (why would you do that to fruit??? fresh is the only way!) but feel free to add some if you are so inclined.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 3 cups oats
  • 3/4 cup pumpkin seeds (or other seeds)
  • 1/2 cup coconut flakes
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil
  • 1/4 cup golden or maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 160 and line a large baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Combine oats, seeds and coconut flakes in a large bowl.
  3. Combine rest of ingredients in a mixing jug. Microwave for 30sec to melt the coconut oil so everything is combined.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and mix with your hands into clumps.
  5. Spread in a single layer onto baking tray. You'll either need to do this on two trays, or in two gos, because you shouldn't have stuff on top of eachother.
  6. Bake for 18 minutes. Repeat with the rest of the granola (unless you're fancy enough to own two trays, in which case you can do it in one go).
  7. Allow the granola to cool completely before storing in an airtight jar or container.
